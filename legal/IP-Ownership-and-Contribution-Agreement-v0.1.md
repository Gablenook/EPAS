# IP Ownership and Contribution Agreement v0.1

**Status:** Business/legal discussion draft; not legal advice; intended for attorney review.  
**Purpose:** Define ownership, contribution, assignment, invention, and limited license principles for collaboration between Kevin / Toren and Fernando / SmartLockerAP / SmartLocker, LLC.

---

## 1. Parties

This draft assumes the parties will include Kevin Booth and/or Toren on one side and Fernando and/or SmartLockerAP, LLC and/or SmartLocker, LLC on the other side. Exact legal party names must be confirmed by counsel.

---

## 2. Purpose

The parties intend to collaborate so that separately owned software platforms can operate together as part of a customer solution.

This Agreement is intended to prevent accidental ownership confusion. It does not create an employment relationship, contractor relationship, partnership, joint venture, co-founder arrangement, advisor arrangement, or vendor relationship.

The intended structure is:

- Kevin / Toren owns EPAS and the Toren Edge Platform.
- Fernando / SmartLockerAP owns the SmartLockerAP backend platform.
- SmartLocker, LLC may sell locker products and may request software licenses from one or more platform owners.
- Penco Products or other channel partners may sell locker products that require the combined software solution.
- Customer-facing licensing may later be simplified, but underlying ownership should remain clear.

---

## 3. No Implied Ownership Transfer

No ownership rights are transferred merely because the parties share information, discuss ideas, review technical materials, participate in meetings, troubleshoot a problem, exchange documents, exchange code, test an integration, or support a customer deployment.

Any ownership transfer, license, assignment, joint ownership arrangement, or commercial sublicensing right must be express and written.

---

## 4. Background IP

### 4.1 Toren Background IP

Toren Background IP includes EPAS, the Toren Edge Platform, Toren platform architecture, Toren edge software, Toren documentation, figures, naming, workflows, source code, compiled components, technical specifications, product hierarchy, deployment model, transaction architecture, commissioning model, commercial packaging, invention materials, and related know-how owned or controlled by Kevin / Toren.

Toren Background IP is further identified in `legal/Toren-Background-IP-Schedule-v0.1.md`.

### 4.2 Fernando / SmartLockerAP Background IP

Fernando / SmartLockerAP Background IP includes SmartLockerAP backend platform assets, backend services, APIs, data models, hosted services, administrative tools, deployment methods, reusable libraries, templates, software methods, development practices, invention materials, and related know-how owned or controlled by Fernando / SmartLockerAP.

Fernando / SmartLockerAP Background IP is further identified in `legal/Fernando-SmartLockerAP-Background-IP-Schedule-v0.1.md`.

### 4.3 Retained Ownership

Each party retains ownership of its Background IP. Collaboration does not convert one party's Background IP into jointly owned IP.

---

## 5. Toren Work Product

Toren Work Product means work created specifically for Toren, EPAS, the Toren Edge Platform, Toren edge software, Toren documentation, Toren diagrams, Toren workflows, Toren architecture, Toren specifications, Toren customer deployments, or Toren commercial packaging.

Toren / Kevin owns Toren Work Product.

To the extent Fernando / SmartLockerAP contributes to Toren Work Product, the final agreement should include attorney-drafted assignment language confirming that such contribution is assigned to Kevin / Toren, except for expressly identified Fernando / SmartLockerAP Background IP.

Examples include:

- EPAS updates;
- Toren Edge Platform specification work;
- Toren edge software;
- Toren kiosk or local-control software;
- Toren workflow definitions;
- Toren transaction, acknowledgement, recovery, and reconciliation models;
- Toren diagrams, figures, and documentation;
- Toren product naming and commercial packaging;
- Toren patent disclosure materials.

---

## 6. SmartLockerAP Work Product

SmartLockerAP Work Product means work created specifically for SmartLockerAP backend platform, backend APIs, backend services, backend infrastructure, backend data model, backend administration, or SmartLockerAP commercial packaging.

Fernando / SmartLockerAP owns SmartLockerAP Work Product.

To the extent Kevin / Toren contributes to SmartLockerAP Work Product, the final agreement should include attorney-drafted assignment language confirming that such contribution is assigned to Fernando / SmartLockerAP, except for expressly identified Toren Background IP.

Examples include:

- backend API implementation;
- backend database schema and migration work;
- backend customer, site, actor, role, permission, kiosk, and locker-bank administration;
- backend asset validation and authorization logic;
- backend acknowledgement and reconciliation services;
- backend hosting, deployment, logging, and monitoring;
- SmartLockerAP administrative tools.

---

## 7. Integration Work Product

Integration Work Product means interface definitions, API contracts, payload structures, field mappings, test plans, diagrams, sample requests, sample responses, and coordination materials created to make Toren / EPAS and SmartLockerAP operate together.

Recommended position:

- Toren owns Toren-side implementation.
- SmartLockerAP owns SmartLockerAP-side implementation.
- Integration documents and interface definitions may be used by both parties for the agreed combined solution.
- Integration work does not give either party ownership of the other party's platform.

The final agreement should decide whether integration artifacts are author-owned, jointly owned only as to the interface layer, or cross-licensed for defined uses.

---

## 8. Limited Licenses for Embedded Background IP

If one party's Background IP is embedded in or necessary to use work product owned by the other party, the contributing party should grant only the limited license needed for the delivered work to function as intended.

The final agreement should define whether that license is:

- non-exclusive or exclusive;
- perpetual or limited;
- sublicensable to customers, manufacturers, dealers, installers, support providers, or channel partners;
- transferable with the related business or assets;
- royalty-free, prepaid, or subject to later commercial license fees;
- limited to the locker / edge platform field;
- limited to specific deployments or customers.

No broad platform license should be implied from technical collaboration alone.

---

## 9. Patentable Inventions

Patentable inventions should be documented separately from ordinary copyrightable work.

Business intent:

- Actual inventorship will be determined under applicable patent law.
- Each party should promptly disclose potentially patentable inventions arising from the collaboration.
- Toren / Kevin should own or control inventions derived primarily from Toren Background IP, EPAS, Toren Edge Platform architecture, Toren edge implementation, or Toren Work Product.
- Fernando / SmartLockerAP should own or control inventions derived primarily from SmartLockerAP Background IP, backend architecture, backend implementation, or SmartLockerAP Work Product.
- Inventions requiring actual inventive contribution from both parties should be documented in a separate invention schedule.
- The parties should cooperate in signing documents reasonably needed to protect agreed ownership and license rights.

Patent filing costs, maintenance costs, prosecution control, enforcement rights, abandonment rights, and foreign filing rights should be addressed by counsel.

---

## 10. Jointly Discussed Ideas

Discussion alone should not create joint ownership.

Ownership should depend on:

- who originated the idea;
- whether the idea derives from either party's Background IP;
- whether the idea is implemented in Toren Work Product, SmartLockerAP Work Product, or Integration Work Product;
- whether the idea becomes part of a patent filing;
- whether the parties later document ownership or license rights in writing.

The parties should avoid casual language suggesting shared ownership unless that is intentional and documented.

---

## 11. Confidentiality and Controlled Use

The Mutual Confidentiality and Non-Use Agreement applies to all exchanged confidential information, including source code, binaries, hosted services, API information, documents, diagrams, customer data, patent disclosures, invention notes, and AI-assisted development materials.

A confidentiality breach or unauthorized use does not create ownership or license rights in the receiving party.

---

## 12. Commercial Licensing Reserved

This Agreement defines ownership and contribution rules. It does not finalize customer-facing software licensing.

A later license agreement should address:

- recurring per-opening software fees;
- Toren commissioning fees;
- SmartLockerAP backend service fees;
- license administration;
- revenue share;
- reporting and audit rights;
- sublicensing to SmartLocker, LLC, Penco Products, customers, installers, or dealers;
- support and maintenance obligations;
- termination and transition for installed customers.

Ownership protection should not wait for commercial license terms to be finalized.

---

## 13. Customer-Specific Customizations

Customer-specific work should be classified when created.

Possible classifications include:

- Toren Work Product;
- SmartLockerAP Work Product;
- Integration Work Product;
- customer-owned work;
- third-party work;
- jointly licensed deployment artifact.

No customer-specific customization should be treated as jointly owned by default.

---

## 14. Third-Party and Open-Source Components

Each party should identify third-party and open-source components included in its Background IP or Work Product.

No party should introduce third-party materials into the other party's platform in a way that creates unwanted source disclosure obligations, patent restrictions, security obligations, or commercial restrictions without written approval.

---

## 15. Records and Cooperation

Each party should maintain records of:

- repository commits;
- authorship;
- invention disclosures;
- source code contributions;
- integration decisions;
- customer-specific customizations;
- third-party components;
- AI-assisted contributions;
- access grants;
- shared confidential materials;
- technical incidents involving protected materials.

Each party should cooperate in signing documents reasonably needed to confirm ownership, assignment, patent filings, license rights, and customer continuity.

---

## 16. End of Collaboration

If collaboration ends:

- each party keeps its Background IP;
- each party keeps Work Product assigned to it;
- confidential information must be returned, deleted, or access-restricted;
- repository, system, credential, and API access must be terminated or reduced as appropriate;
- deployed customer rights should continue according to the applicable license agreement;
- pending inventions, support obligations, and transition issues should be resolved in writing.

---

## 17. Open Legal Terms for Counsel

Counsel should complete or confirm:

- exact party names and signatories;
- whether Kevin owns IP personally or through Toren;
- whether SmartLockerAP, LLC, SmartLocker, LLC, and/or Fernando personally must sign;
- governing law and venue;
- assignment wording;
- patent assignment wording;
- consideration;
- sublicensing rights;
- affiliate rights;
- indemnities;
- limitation of liability;
- dispute resolution;
- injunctive relief;
- survival;
- customer transition language;
- export, cybersecurity, privacy, and data-processing terms if needed.

---

## 18. Plain-English Summary

Toren owns Toren. SmartLockerAP owns SmartLockerAP. Work created specifically for Toren belongs to Toren. Work created specifically for SmartLockerAP belongs to SmartLockerAP. Integration work lets the systems operate together, but it should not make either party the owner of the other party's platform.
