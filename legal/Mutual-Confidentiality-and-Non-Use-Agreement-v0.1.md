# Mutual Confidentiality and Non-Use Agreement v0.1

**Purpose:** Protect confidential information exchanged between Kevin / Toren and Fernando / SmartLockerAP / SmartLocker, LLC while allowing technical collaboration.

## 1. Parties

This Mutual Confidentiality and Non-Use Agreement is intended to be entered into by the following parties, with exact legal names to be confirmed by counsel:

- Kevin Booth and/or Toren, as owner or controller of EPAS and the Toren Edge Platform; and
- Fernando and/or SmartLockerAP, LLC and/or SmartLocker, LLC, as owner or controller of SmartLockerAP backend platform assets and SmartLocker commercial activities.

Each party may be referred to as a “Disclosing Party” when disclosing Confidential Information and as a “Receiving Party” when receiving Confidential Information.

---

## 2. Purpose

The parties wish to collaborate on interoperating locker, edge platform, backend platform, and customer deployment solutions.

The purpose of this Agreement is to allow the parties to share technical, business, customer, and implementation information while preventing either party from using the other party's Confidential Information outside the defined collaboration.

This Agreement is intended to protect both sides. It is not intended to transfer ownership of either party's IP.

---

## 3. No Contractor, Employee, Co-Founder, Advisor, Vendor, or Partnership Relationship

The parties acknowledge that their intended relationship is collaboration between separate platform owners.

Nothing in this Agreement makes either party an employee, contractor, co-founder, advisor, vendor, partner, franchisee, agent, fiduciary, or joint venturer of the other party.

No party may bind the other party or represent that it owns, controls, or may sublicense the other party's IP except as expressly authorized in a signed written agreement.

---

## 4. Confidential Information

“Confidential Information” means any non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Disclosing Party to a Receiving Party, whether disclosed orally, visually, electronically, in writing, through repositories, through hosted systems, through AI tools, through demonstrations, through customer deployments, through source code, through binaries, or through any other means.

Confidential Information includes, without limitation:

- source code;
- binaries, DLLs, executable files, services, and build outputs;
- APIs, API internals, schemas, payloads, DTOs, endpoints, and integration contracts;
- specifications, including EPAS and non-public specification drafts;
- architecture diagrams, figures, workflows, and implementation diagrams;
- database schemas and data models;
- transaction, authorization, ACK, recovery, and reconciliation models;
- customer, site, location, kiosk, locker-bank, and asset information;
- deployment information;
- credentials, keys, tokens, certificates, and secrets;
- logs and diagnostics containing non-public information;
- patent disclosures, invention notes, claim concepts, and invention diagrams;
- pricing, licensing, commissioning fees, revenue-share concepts, and commercial strategy;
- business plans, product plans, naming, packaging, and roadmap information;
- AI prompts and AI outputs containing or derived from Confidential Information;
- repository content and issue discussions not clearly intended for unrestricted public use;
- any information that should reasonably be understood to be confidential based on its nature or context.

---

## 5. Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can prove by written records:

1. was already lawfully known to the Receiving Party before disclosure;
2. was publicly available through no breach of this Agreement;
3. was lawfully received from a third party without confidentiality restrictions;
4. was independently developed without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or
5. was approved for release in a signed written authorization by the Disclosing Party.

The burden should be on the Receiving Party to prove an exclusion applies.

---

## 6. Non-Use Obligation

The Receiving Party may use Confidential Information only for the defined collaboration purpose.

Unless expressly authorized in a signed written agreement, the Receiving Party may not use Confidential Information to:

- build, copy, clone, replace, commercialize, or compete with the Disclosing Party's platform;
- file or support patent applications based on the Disclosing Party's inventions or confidential technical concepts;
- train, prompt, fine-tune, or instruct AI systems;
- reverse engineer, reconstruct, decompile, disassemble, or derive source code, architecture, or algorithms;
- create derivative works outside the agreed collaboration;
- solicit customers using the Disclosing Party's non-public customer or deployment information;
- bypass the Disclosing Party in licensing or customer relationships;
- disclose or sublicense the Disclosing Party's IP to Penco Products, SmartLocker, LLC, SmartLockerAP, customers, installers, vendors, or channel partners unless expressly authorized.

---

## 7. Non-Disclosure Obligation

The Receiving Party must protect Confidential Information using at least reasonable care and no less care than it uses to protect its own similar confidential information.

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to its representatives who:

- need to know the information for the defined collaboration purpose;
- are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement; and
- are not competitors or unauthorized third parties.

The Receiving Party remains responsible for misuse or unauthorized disclosure by its representatives.

---

## 8. Repository, Source Code, Binary, and System Access

Access to repositories, source code, binaries, DLLs, hosted services, APIs, test systems, customer environments, logs, or deployment systems is limited to the specific purpose for which access was granted.

Access does not create ownership, assignment, license, implied license, joint ownership, work-made-for-hire status, or sublicensing rights.

The Receiving Party must not exceed the granted access scope.

---

## 9. Reverse Engineering, Decompilation, and Inspection Restrictions

Unless expressly authorized in advance in a signed written authorization for a specific purpose, the Receiving Party may not:

- reverse engineer;
- decompile;
- disassemble;
- inspect internal logic;
- reconstruct source code;
- derive data structures;
- derive algorithms;
- derive architecture;
- extract proprietary methods;
- use AI tools to infer, reconstruct, explain, or regenerate proprietary source code or logic;
- use logs, binaries, DLLs, APIs, payloads, or services to create a substitute implementation.

This restriction applies to Toren components, SmartLockerAP components, SmartLocker components, and any third-party components disclosed under the collaboration.

---

## 10. Accidental Reverse Engineering or Unauthorized AI Exposure

If reverse engineering, decompilation, unauthorized inspection, or unauthorized AI-assisted analysis occurs accidentally, during troubleshooting, or without proper authorization, the Receiving Party must promptly:

1. notify the Disclosing Party;
2. stop the activity;
3. preserve relevant evidence;
4. identify what was accessed, uploaded, generated, inferred, copied, or derived;
5. segregate any resulting materials;
6. not use resulting materials except to investigate or remediate the incident;
7. delete, return, or destroy resulting materials if requested by the Disclosing Party;
8. cooperate in remediation and prevention.

Good-faith troubleshooting may explain why an incident occurred, but it does not create ownership, license, or reuse rights.

---

## 11. AI Tool Restrictions

The Receiving Party may not upload, paste, submit, train on, fine-tune with, or otherwise provide the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information to AI tools unless the parties have approved in writing:

- the specific AI tool or service;
- the categories of information that may be used;
- whether the tool retains, trains on, or shares input data;
- whether customer data may be included;
- whether source code, binaries, DLLs, patent disclosures, or credentials are prohibited;
- required redaction procedures;
- retention and deletion procedures.

AI-generated output based on Confidential Information remains subject to this Agreement.

---

## 12. Patent and Invention Confidentiality

Patent disclosures, invention notes, claim concepts, diagrams, technical novelty discussions, filing strategy, and prosecution strategy are Confidential Information.

The Receiving Party may not use the Disclosing Party's invention information to file, amend, support, or challenge a patent application without written authorization.

The parties should separately document actual inventorship, assignment obligations, prosecution rights, license rights, and filing-cost responsibilities in the IP Ownership and Contribution Agreement.

---

## 13. No License or Transfer

No license, assignment, ownership transfer, joint ownership, work-made-for-hire status, or other IP right is granted by this Agreement except the limited right to use Confidential Information for the defined collaboration purpose.

All other rights are reserved.

---

## 14. Required Disclosures

If the Receiving Party is legally required to disclose Confidential Information, the Receiving Party must, to the extent legally permitted:

- promptly notify the Disclosing Party;
- cooperate in seeking protective treatment;
- disclose only the minimum required information;
- continue to treat the information as confidential.

---

## 15. Return, Deletion, and Access Termination

Upon request or upon termination of collaboration, the Receiving Party must return, delete, or destroy Confidential Information, including copies, extracts, notes, downloads, local clones, AI outputs, generated summaries, and derivative materials, except that one archival copy may be retained solely for legal compliance if required.

Repository, system, API, and credential access must be terminated when no longer needed.

---

## 16. Survival

Confidentiality and non-use obligations should survive for at least five years after disclosure.

For trade secrets, source code, unreleased patent disclosures, credentials, and highly sensitive technical or business information, obligations should continue for as long as the information remains non-public and legally protectable.

---

## 17. Equitable Relief

The parties acknowledge that unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm that cannot be adequately remedied by money damages alone.

The Disclosing Party should be entitled to seek injunctive or equitable relief in addition to any other available remedies.

---

## 18. No Obligation to Proceed

This Agreement does not require either party to enter into a commercial agreement, license agreement, customer agreement, development agreement, patent agreement, or other transaction.

---

## 20. Plain-English Summary

Each side can share information to make Toren / EPAS and SmartLockerAP work together. But neither side can use the other side's confidential material to clone, replace, reverse engineer, patent around, train AI, or commercially bypass the other side's platform.

The goal is safe collaboration, not accidental ownership transfer.
